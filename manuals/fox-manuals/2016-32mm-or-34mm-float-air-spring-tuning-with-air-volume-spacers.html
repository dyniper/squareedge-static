SERVICE PROCEDURES

2016 32mm/34mm FLOAT Air Spring Tuning with Air Volume Spacers

Jump to step: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5

Required Tools

  • 027-00-013 or 027-00-018 Shock pump
  • 398-00-702 Tooling: Fork Topcap Socket, 26mm, 3/8 Drive

WARNING: Always wear safety glasses and protective gloves during service to prevent potential injury. Failure to wear protective equipment during service may lead to SERIOUS INJURY OR DEATH.

Changing volume spacers in the 32/34 FLOAT fork is an easy internal adjustment that allows you to change the amount of mid stroke and bottom out resistance.

  • If you have set your sag correctly and are using full travel (bottoming out) too easily, then you could install one or more spacers to increase bottom out resistance.

  • If you have set your sag correctly and are not using full travel, then you could remove one or more spacers to decrease bottom out resistance.

Step 1 top

Remove the blue air cap and cover the air valve with a rag as you release the air pressure.



WARNING: Please verify that all air has been released from the air chamber by pushing down on the Schrader valve core. Failure to release all air pressure before further disassembly may cause parts to eject causing SEVERE INJURY OR DEATH.

Step 2 top

With a chamfer-less 6-point 26mm socket (PN: 398-00-602), unthread the topcap assembly completely (counter-clockwise). Pull straight up to remove the entire topcap assembly from the left side upper tube. Some upward force might be needed to overcome the friction of the stock air volume spacer.

NOTE: 32mm fork shown with largest of 3 hollow volume spacers, use the same procedure for 34mm forks.



Step 3 top

Slide the air volume spacer or spacers horizontally to install or remove from the topcap. 

NOTE: 2 32mm volume spacers are being removed in this example.



DO NOT remove the hollow volume spacer from the topcap on 32mm forks. This spacer must be utilized if it came stock in your fork.

For 34mm forks, when using 6 or more air volume spacers, you must use the green 34mm air volume spacer (PN: 234-04-953) in the 6th and lower positions to prevent rattling in the larger internal diameter portion of the upper tube. 

WARNING: DO NOT exceed the maximum number or volume spacers listed in the tables below, as this can damage your fork causing a loss of control of the bicycle leading to SERIOUS INJURY or DEATH.

Step 4 top

Reinstall the topcap into the fork crown and tighten clockwise to 220 in-lb (24.8 Nm) torque with your 6-point chamfer-less 26mm socket.



Step 5 top

Add air pressure to your desired setting using a FOX high pressure pump. See the Setting Fork Air Pressure section for more information. Reinstall the blue air cap then go ride!
